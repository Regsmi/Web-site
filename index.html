Two 'girls' in the toon. Things to do in Newcastle

Nightlife activities

TigerTiger: "Great place.Central location. Famous with it's student nights every Wednesday. Has 3 different dancefloors with 3 different styles of music. A must to attend place for people who enjoys dressing up and just having fun in a good company."

Flares: "It is a great place to dance and sing along to all of the throwbacks. Some nights present music from 60's to 90's. Great atmospehere, affordable drinks and amazing DJ."

The Empress: "Free entry every night of the week. Open until 3am, which is longer than any other club in town. Pop and RnB music with great drink deals."

Bierkeller: "Oktoberfest all year round. German inspired pub in the central location of the city. If dancing on the benches and drinking 2 pint beers at once is your thing, come and join the festive mood any time of the year."

Daytime activities

Quilliam Brothers (QB): "Sick of drinking boring, dull and often over-brewed tea in one of the unexciting chains? Give the experts in tea a try. And cake, of course. And their English breakfast, why not?"

Cake Stories: "Located right by West Jesmond metro, Cake Stories is a must visit for those with a sweet tooth. You can find the perfect cake for every taste: from zesty lemon drizzle to the naughtiest chocolate-oreo cookie monster."

Grainger Market: "180 year-old market right in the heart of the city. Not only can you do your groceries shopping at local businesses and traders, but have some food on site (dumplings featured)."

Tynemouth: "With easy access by metro -only 25 minutes away- Tynemouth is a very good option if you are looking for a fun day out. Don't forget to be hungry to enjoy fish and chips to the fullest!"
